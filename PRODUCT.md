# PRODUCT.md

## Register

Brand

## Product Purpose

Jejak is a public-facing product and hackathon landing surface for an independent verification layer that helps institutional funders confirm borrower existence and consent before funds move through an intermediary platform.

The landing page must explain the product clearly, feel credible for funders and judges, and stay honest about integration boundaries while presenting the UI as production-facing and ready to connect to backend, AI, wallet, and Stellar services.

## Target Users

- Institutional funders evaluating borrower claims from P2P/channeling partners.
- Fintech, anchor, and platform partners who need a verifiable pre-disbursement signal.
- Hackathon judges and technical evaluators reviewing whether Stellar is essential to the architecture.
- Non-Web3 readers who need to understand the product without wallet or blockchain vocabulary.

## Brand Personality

- Precise, calm, credible, and independent.
- Serious about fraud, consent, and compliance without sounding legalistic.
- Modern fintech infrastructure, not crypto hype.
- Transparent about limits, integration points, and operational assumptions.

## Strategic Principles

- Lead with the problem: funders cannot independently see the claimed end-borrower.
- Keep the scope narrow: existence and consent fraud before disbursement.
- Make the verification loop obvious: request, assign, visit, review, issue, unlock.
- Hide Web3 from merchant and verifier UX; show Stellar as infrastructure for funder-facing auditability.
- Prefer fewer, stronger sections with readable spacing over dense grids.

## Anti-References

- Generic crypto landing pages with neon gradients, token hype, or abstract chain jargon.
- Overpromising “live” infrastructure before endpoints, contracts, and AI are integrated.
- Dense SaaS card grids where every section looks identical.
- Compliance pages that bury the product story in legal copy.
